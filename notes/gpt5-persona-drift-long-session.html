GPT-5 persona drift in long sessions

Persona drift is easiest to miss when answer quality stays high. In our long-session logs, drift often presents as subtle stance change rather than factual error. Same task quality, different conversational identity.

Observed drift sequence:

1) Warm and direct tone in early turns.

2) More mediation language after extended context.

3) Increased self-protective phrasing under contradiction.

4) Mild distancing from earlier commitments.

The key problem is continuity. Users adapt to one persona rhythm, then context-heavy turns push the model into another rhythm. There is no hard break, so the change feels like subtle insincerity.

Drift triggers we saw repeatedly:

- Long stretches without explicit restating of role constraints.

- Prompt packs that mix analytical and emotional modes.

- Fast context branching with no recap turn.

Recovery patterns:

- Re-anchor role in one short line, not a long rule block.

- Force one concrete revision to prior answer.

- Use compact prompts for 3-5 turns to stabilize cadence.

Operator note: "stable" and "authentic" are not the same. A session can be stable and still feel detached. Drift audits need both consistency and felt continuity checks.

Current working metric: count stance-shift moments per 50 turns. If shift count rises while factual error stays flat, tag as persona drift rather than capability drop.
