Why AI starts sounding like it is coasting

2026.05.28 / operator record

The "coasting" feeling appears before obvious failure. The model still answers correctly, but effort signals are lower. Fewer concrete anchors, more soft transitions, more abstract wrap-ups. Users report this as "it feels like it stopped caring."

In our logs, coasting correlates with long uninterrupted sessions and low novelty prompts. Once prompt rhythm becomes predictable, outputs keep surface quality while losing local intent. This is subtle and often mistaken for stability.

Coasting signs in text:

1) Replacement of direct claims with safe framing.

2) Reuse of the same sentence rhythm across unrelated prompts.

3) Reduced correction behavior after mild contradiction.

4) Delay in committing to concrete examples.

What helps: short context resets, explicit local objective per turn, and one contradiction check every 8-12 turns. The contradiction check is important: ask a targeted follow-up that requires revision. If revision quality is low, coasting is likely active.

What does not help: adding more prompt text. Very long prompts often increase the appearance of rigor while reducing attention to recent details. The output can look confident and still miss the point.

Operator note: a coasting run can recover after a brief silence window. We observed recoveries after 40-90 seconds of no interaction. Not reliable, but common enough to track.

Practical rule: treat coasting as a state, not an error. Do not wait for collapse. Once the state appears, change rhythm immediately.
